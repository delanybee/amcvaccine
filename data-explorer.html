The case in five charts

Each visualization builds on the last, tracing the path from market failure to measurable climate impact. The data behind the narrative.

1 / The problem

Innovation stalls when the downside is unbounded

Without guaranteed demand, an R&D firm faces a wide distribution of outcomes that includes deep losses. The expected value might be positive, but the probability of catastrophic loss keeps capital on the sidelines. This is the core market failure: rational firms decline rational investments because the risk profile is asymmetric.

Firm payoff distribution: before and after AMC

Probability curves for R&D project payoff under two scenarios. The AMC truncates downside exposure below -$20M.

Key insight: The AMC does not increase expected returns. It reshapes the distribution by guaranteeing a floor, which is often more important to investment committees than raising the ceiling.
If the risk profile changes, so does the adoption math. The next chart shows what happens to farmer participation when economics shift.

2 / The shift

AMC moves the adoption curve to the right

Producer adoption follows a logistic curve: at low cost most farmers participate, at high cost almost none do. The AMC effectively subsidizes the cost threshold, shifting the entire curve rightward. The shaded area between curves is the adoption surplus directly attributable to the commitment mechanism.

Adoption frontier with and without AMC

Modeled using logistic functions fitted to rancher survey data. Crossover costs estimated from USDA per-animal treatment benchmarks.

Design implication: The width of the shaded region is a policy design variable. AMC subsidy depth directly controls how far right the curve shifts.
Adoption happens in specific places. Readiness varies dramatically by country, which shapes where an AMC lands first.

3 / The geography

Readiness is uneven, but the opportunity is global

Adoption readiness is a composite index weighing herd size, veterinary infrastructure, regulatory capacity, producer organization, and climate policy alignment. High readiness does not guarantee adoption, but it predicts where early deployment is feasible. Brazil and India lead on herd scale; the U.S. and Australia lead on infrastructure. The gap between them is where AMC design choices matter most.

Global adoption readiness index

Composite score (0-100) across 20 livestock-producing nations. Hover for country details.

Geography tells us where. The next question is how much funding translates into how much impact.

4 / The returns

Funding scales sub-linearly: the first dollars matter most

The relationship between AMC funding and methane abatement follows a concave curve. Early funding unlocks the cheapest abatement opportunities. Beyond a threshold, each additional dollar buys less impact because marginal herds are harder to reach, infrastructure costs rise, and adoption friction increases. This means AMC sizing is a strategic choice, not a simple multiplier.

Funding vs. cumulative abatement

Estimated MtCO2e abated as AMC commitment size increases. The $750M target sits in the high-impact region before the curve flattens.

The $750M target: Chosen to capture the steep part of the curve. Moving to $1.2B adds only ~14% more abatement while increasing costs by 60%.
All these inputs compound over time. The cumulative picture reveals why near-term action on methane carries outsized returns.

5 / The payoff

Cumulative avoided emissions diverge rapidly

Because methane is short-lived but potent, early reductions have a disproportionate effect on near-term warming. The area between the two curves represents emissions avoided specifically because the AMC accelerated deployment. By 2035, the gap is nearly 150 MtCO2e. That wedge is the return on the policy commitment.

Cumulative emissions reduction: with and without AMC

Projected avoided emissions (MtCO2e) under moderate adoption and baseline dosing assumptions, 2027-2035.

Time value of methane: A ton of methane reduced in 2028 prevents more warming than the same reduction in 2033. The AMC's value is not just the total abatement but that it pulls the timeline forward.
