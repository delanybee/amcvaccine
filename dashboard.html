About This Dashboard

This dashboard is a compact scenario explorer for an advance market commitment, or AMC. It is designed to help a policy audience see how results move when a few economically important assumptions change, without pretending that the model is more precise than it is.

Methane and cattle: Enteric methane from cattle is a greenhouse gas source. Reducing methane can create climate benefits if a vaccine lowers emissions and is adopted at scale.
AMC: An advance market commitment is a demand-side incentive. It promises a future market if an eligible product is successfully developed.
BCR: Benefit-cost ratio compares modeled social benefits to modeled social costs. Higher than 1 means benefits exceed costs under the assumptions shown.
NPV: Net present value here is simplified to adjusted benefits minus the AMC component, following the requested presentation logic.
Eta: Eta is scientific feasibility. Lower eta means it is harder to hit the target success probability, which raises the implied required firm-level success probability.
Document-derived vs stress-tested: Baseline and annual quantities come from provided figures; prime plus booster and the eta-based AMC rule are explicit exploratory extensions.
