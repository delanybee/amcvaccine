Step 1: Scientific feasibility (eta)

Higher eta means the technical pathway is more reliable. In this model, greater feasibility lowers implied risk and improves benefit-cost performance.

Step 2: Adoption rate

Adoption is often the strongest practical lever. Even strong science underperforms if producer uptake remains low.

Step 3: Social value of methane reduction

Higher social value raises the modeled climate benefit. This can shift a borderline design into a high-return policy case.
